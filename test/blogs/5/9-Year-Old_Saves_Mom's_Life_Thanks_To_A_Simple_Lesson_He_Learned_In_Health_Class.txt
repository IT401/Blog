<p>When single Washington mom-of-three Kimberly Smith was caring for her 4-week-old baby, she began to experience chest pains, shortness of breath, and difficulty breathing. She attributed the symptoms to her recent bout of pneumonia, and thought if she just put the newborn down and took a nap, she'd feel better. If she had done that, however, the results may have been fatal, according to doctors.</p>
<p>"He said, 'My health teacher said that when someone grabs their chest like that that I'm supposed to call 911,'" reports The Huffington Post.</p>
<p>The little boy explained to told KING 5 in Washington state, "I kept saying that we needed to go. So, I just went and got my shoes and coat on, and told her we needed to go."</p>
<p>There was something about the conviction with which the fourth grader urged her to seek help, that Smith decided go to the doctor. After an EKG test, she was rushed to the hospital for further testing. Upon hearing her diagnosis, she now credits her young son for saving her life.</p>
